## 2. Smart Manager for WooCommerce

**Category:** Established plugin
**Installs:** 10,000+
**Price:** $79/yr

### What it does

A spreadsheet-style grid with inline editing. Works with products, orders, and customers. A mature product with years of development behind it.

### Where it excels

Solid foundation and broad WooCommerce support. Handles products, orders, and customers in one place. The premium version is capable and the team responds to support requests.

### Where it falls short

The free version is capped at 10 products per page. That's not a free version — it's a demo you have to pay to remove. Variation editing is also premium-only, which is the #1 complaint in 1-star reviews on WordPress.org.

Performance degrades noticeably past 200-300 products. The interface works but feels heavy compared to newer alternatives.

### Common complaints from 1-star reviews

- "Free version shows only 10 products at a time. What's the point?"
- "Slow with 500+ products. Times out regularly."
- "Variations are a premium feature? That's the main reason I needed bulk editing."

### Bottom line

A reasonable option if you need to edit orders and customers alongside products, and you're willing to pay for the premium tier. For pure product bulk editing, there are better values.

## 3. Bulk Editor for WooCommerce (BEAR)

**Category:** Established plugin
**Installs:** 8,000+
**Price:** $69/yr

### What it does

A two-pane editor: a filter panel on the left, a data grid on the right. Promises inline editing for products, with a focus on speed.

### Where it excels

BEAR has a loyal user base. The interface is more structured than some competitors, which some users prefer. For simple bulk operations (price updates, stock changes), it works reliably.

### Where it falls short

The free version is limited to editing 10 products per page — same problem as Smart Manager. Preview mode is not available in the free version. Undo is premium-only. Variation editing is premium-only.

Performance is acceptable up to 200 products but degrades beyond that. The UI was built for desktop — on smaller screens it becomes difficult to use.

### Common complaints from 1-star reviews

- "After 200 products the editor becomes nearly unusable."
- "No preview means you don't know what will happen until you click save."
- "Trying to edit variations in the free version just shows a locked interface."

### Bottom line

The premium version is capable. The free version is not representative of the product. Evaluate the premium with a trial before committing.

## 4. ELEX WooCommerce Bulk Edit Products

**Category:** Established plugin
**Installs:** 5,000+
**Price:** $49/yr

A plugin focused specifically on bulk product editing. Includes a form-based bulk editor alongside a spreadsheet view.

- "Interface is confusing. Not clear which fields you're editing until you save."

### Bottom line

The premium version is mid-range priced. The free version is insulting. There is no reason to choose this over alternatives that offer functional free versions.
