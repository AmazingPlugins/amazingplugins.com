## Pitch Email

**Subject:** How-to: Making a WooCommerce Store WCAG 2.1 AA Compliant (no overlay required)

Hi A11Y Project team,

I'd love to contribute a How-to article on making WooCommerce stores WCAG 2.1 AA compliant without using an overlay widget.

**Why this topic matters:**
WooCommerce powers over 30% of all e-commerce sites, and most of them fail basic accessibility checks. Store owners get demand letters under the ADA/EAA, panic, and reach for an overlay. Courts have rejected overlays as compliance evidence. There's almost no practical, code-level guidance for this specific platform.

**Proposed title:** How to Make Your WooCommerce Store WCAG 2.1 AA Compliant (Without an Overlay)

**What the article covers:**
- Why WooCommerce sites are notoriously inaccessible (keyboard traps in variant pickers, missing alt text, unlabeled checkout forms, poor color contrast)
- How to audit a WooCommerce store using WAVE or Accessibility Insights
- The specific fixes that matter most: alt text automation, keyboard navigation in product variants, form label association, focus indicator visibility, skip links, heading hierarchy
- Why code-level fixes are legally defensible in a way overlays are not
- A note on the free vs. pro landscape (including what AmazingPlugins offers as a reference, but focused on actionable manual steps too)

**Why I'm qualified:**
I run AmazingPlugins, where we build code-level accessibility fixers for WooCommerce and Shopify. I've audited dozens of WooCommerce stores and have a clear picture of what the most common violations are and what actually works to fix them.

I'm happy to follow the A11Y Project content style guide and keep it accessible for newcomers.

Would this be a good fit? Happy to adjust the angle if you think there's a better framing for your readers.

Best,
