## 1. Brand Positioning (amazingplugins.com)

**Primary:**
"I build WordPress plugins that actually work. No crippled free versions, no bait-and-switch pricing. Just tools that save you time."

**Alternate (shorter, for footers/taglines):**
"WordPress plugins built by a developer who uses them."

Use the primary on the homepage. Use the alternate in email signatures, plugin footers, and social bios. Both are founder-voice, not agency-speak. They set the tone: this is one person shipping quality tools, not a faceless company.

## 2. Landing Page Structure (amazingplugins.com)

The homepage serves two jobs at pre-launch: (1) establish the brand, (2) drive signups for the first plugin. Keep it to one page, no navigation beyond anchor links.

### Section 1: Hero

- Headline (see options below)
- Subheadline: one sentence expanding the headline
- Animated GIF or static mockup of the spreadsheet editor in action (price editing, variation rows expanding)
- CTA button: "Get Early Access" (scrolls to signup form)
- Trust line below CTA: "Free plugin. No credit card. Launching [month]."

### Section 2: Pain Statement

Short, 2-3 sentences. No bullet points.

> "WooCommerce's built-in bulk editor times out, loses changes, and cannot touch variations. You end up editing products one by one. That is not bulk editing. That is data entry."

### Section 3: What Makes This Different (3-column feature grid)

Three cards, each with an icon, a bold statement, and one supporting sentence:

1. **Free version that works** — "Edit all core fields, including variations, without paying. Not a demo. Not 10-products-per-page. A real tool."
2. **Preview before you save** — "See exactly what will change before committing. Red for old values, green for new. No more praying after a bulk update."
3. **Built-in undo** — "Made a mistake? Roll it back in one click. Your data is safe. Always."

### Section 4: Competitor Comparison (Optional, High Impact)

Simple table. No logos, no competitor bashing. Just facts.

| Edit variations in free version | Yes | 10/page limit | Premium only | Premium only |

| Works with 5,000+ products | Yes | Slow | Slow | Untested |

*Footer note: "Competitor names withheld. Data from public WordPress.org reviews, April 2026."*

Not a full pricing table (the product does not exist yet). Just a sentence:

## 4. Best Signup CTA

### Button Copy

**Primary:** "Save My Spot"
**Alternate:** "Get Early Access"

Do not use "Subscribe" or "Sign Up" -- those are generic and signal newsletter, not exclusive access. "Save My Spot" creates mild urgency without being manipulative.

### Form Fields

- **Email** (required, obviously)
- **Question 1:** "How many products does your WooCommerce store have?" (dropdown: Under 50 / 50-500 / 500-2,000 / 2,000+)
- **Question 2:** "What is your biggest frustration with managing products in bulk?" (open text, optional, max 200 chars)

### Why These Two Questions

**Question 1** segments the list by store size. Store size predicts willingness to pay: stores with 500+ products are 3-5x more likely to convert to premium. This data shapes launch messaging (different email copy for small vs. large stores) and tells you whether you are attracting the right audience. If 80% of signups have under 50 products, the landing page is attracting hobbyists, not buyers.

**Question 2** gives you voice-of-customer language for launch copy. Real words from real store owners are better than anything you write. If 15 people say "variations are a nightmare," that phrase goes in the launch email subject line. If they say "I waste 3 hours every Monday updating prices," that goes on the landing page.

Do not add more questions. Every additional field reduces signup completion rate. Two is the maximum for a pre-launch form.

## 5. Fourteen-Day Validation Plan

### Goal

By day 14, have enough signal to decide: build the MVP (go) or revisit the concept (no-go).

### Go/No-Go Gate (Day 14)

| Metric | Go | Caution | No-Go |
|---|---|---|---|
| Email signups | 25+ | 10-24 | Under 10 |
| Community engagement (comments + upvotes across all posts) | 30+ | 10-29 | Under 10 |
| Open-text responses mentioning variations or bulk pricing pain | 5+ | 2-4 | 0-1 |
| Competitor complaint catalog (documented from reviews) | 50+ complaints | 30-49 | Under 30 |

If you hit "Go" on signups AND engagement: proceed to MVP build (5-week timeline per decision brief).
If "Caution" on one metric but "Go" on others: extend validation by 7 days, double down on the weak channel.
If "No-Go" on two or more: pivot to the Accessibility Fixer or Sale Scheduler.

**Actions:**
- [ ] Set up amazingplugins.com on Cloudflare Pages or Vercel (static HTML, no WordPress needed for the landing page itself)
- [ ] Build a single page with the structure from Section 2 above
- [ ] Use headline #2 as default: "Bulk edit WooCommerce products like a spreadsheet. Variations included. Free."
- [ ] Create a static mockup or wireframe screenshot of the spreadsheet grid (does not need to be a working product)
- [ ] Connect email signup to ConvertKit, Buttondown, or any provider that supports a custom form with two extra fields
- [ ] Install Plausible.io or Fathom analytics (privacy-first, no cookie banner needed)
- [ ] Set up a goal/event for CTA button clicks and form submissions

**Time:** 4-6 hours.

### Day 2-3: Competitor Review Mining

**Actions:**
- [ ] Read every 1-star and 2-star review on WordPress.org for: Smart Manager, PW Bulk Edit, BEAR, Bulky, ELEX, WP Sheet Editor, YITH Bulk Product Editing
- [ ] Copy each complaint into a spreadsheet with columns: Plugin, Review Date, Complaint Category, Exact Quote
- [ ] Categorize complaints: Crippled Free Version / Variation Editing Broken / Performance / Data Corruption / UI/UX Confusion / Pricing / Support Quality
- [ ] Count totals per category
- [ ] Highlight the 10 most vivid, quotable complaints (these become marketing ammunition)

**Target:** 50+ documented complaints across all plugins.
**Time:** 3-4 hours total.

### Day 4-5: Reddit Research Posts (NOT promotion)

**Post 1 — r/woocommerce (Day 4):**
Title: "Store owners with 200+ products: what do you use for bulk editing?"
Body: "I manage a store with [X] products and the built-in WooCommerce editor is painful. Tried a couple of plugins but they all seem to lock useful features behind paywalls. What are you using? Anything that handles variations without requiring a premium upgrade?"

Rules: Do NOT mention AmazingPlugins. Do NOT link to your site. This is a genuine research post. Read and engage with every reply. Note which competitors get praised and which get criticized. Save the thread URL.

**Post 2 — r/WordPress (Day 5):**
Title: "Anyone found a WooCommerce bulk editor where the free version isn't crippled?"
Body: "Looking for something that lets me edit prices and variations without paying $200/year. The free versions I've tried are basically demos. Am I missing something, or is this just how WooCommerce plugins work?"

Same rules. No self-promotion. Engage authentically with replies.

**Target:** 20+ combined comments/upvotes across both posts.
**Time:** 30 minutes to write each post, then 10 minutes/day engaging with replies through day 14.

### Day 6-7: Facebook Group Research

**Actions:**
- [ ] Join "WooCommerce Help & Share" (100K+ members) and "WooCommerce Developers & Users"
- [ ] Search each group for "bulk edit," "bulk update prices," "edit variations," and "spreadsheet editor"
- [ ] Screenshot every relevant thread from the last 12 months
- [ ] Note which plugins get recommended and which get criticized
- [ ] Do NOT post yet -- observe only in week 1. Groups are more suspicious of new members posting immediately.

**Target:** 10+ relevant threads documented.
**Time:** 2 hours total.

### Day 8: WordPress.org Forum Mining

**Actions:**
- [ ] Search the WooCommerce support forums (wordpress.org/support/plugin/woocommerce/) for "bulk edit," "edit variations," "bulk update"
- [ ] Search support forums of competing plugins for unresolved issues and feature requests
- [ ] Document the top 10 unresolved support threads where a user is asking for something your plugin would solve
- [ ] Note exact phrasing users employ (this is SEO keyword research from real users)

**Target:** 10 unresolved threads documented.
**Time:** 1.5 hours.

### Day 9-10: Comparison Blog Post Draft

**Actions:**
- [ ] Write "The 6 Best WooCommerce Bulk Edit Plugins Compared (2026 Honest Review)"
- [ ] 2,500+ words. Evaluate each competitor honestly: what they do well, what they do poorly, pricing, free version quality
- [ ] Include a comparison table with: plugin name, price, free version quality (1-5 stars), variation editing (yes/no/limited), undo (yes/no), performance rating
- [ ] Position AmazingPlugins Bulk Editor as "coming soon" at the end, with a link to the signup page
- [ ] Optimize for "best woocommerce bulk edit plugin 2026" -- use the phrase naturally 8-12 times
- [ ] Publish on amazingplugins.com/blog/

**Target:** Published and indexed by Google by day 10.
**Time:** 4-5 hours to write, 30 minutes to publish.

### Day 11-12: Community Engagement (Warm)

**Actions:**
- [ ] If the Reddit posts from day 4-5 got traction, reply to commenters with genuine follow-up questions about their workflow
- [ ] Post in one WooCommerce Facebook group: "I've been researching WooCommerce bulk editors and compiled an honest comparison of the 6 main options. Happy to share if anyone's interested." Link to the blog post. This is value-first, not self-promotion.
- [ ] Search r/woocommerce for any new "bulk edit" threads posted in the last week. Reply helpfully. If your comparison post is relevant, link it.
- [ ] Check email signups daily. Note the open-text responses.

**Time:** 1 hour total.

### Day 13: YouTube Search Check

**Actions:**
- [ ] Search YouTube for "woocommerce bulk edit" and "woocommerce bulk edit tutorial"
- [ ] Note the top 5 videos: view counts, upload dates, which plugins they feature
- [ ] Identify whether a 3-5 minute demo video would fill a gap (likely yes -- most existing videos are 2+ years old)
- [ ] Do NOT record a video yet (no product to demo). Just note this as a day-1-of-MVP action item.

**Time:** 30 minutes.

### Day 14: Validation Gate

**Actions:**
- [ ] Pull email signup count from ConvertKit/Buttondown
- [ ] Pull Reddit post engagement metrics (upvotes + comments)
- [ ] Review open-text responses from signup form -- categorize by pain type
- [ ] Review competitor complaint catalog -- confirm 50+ documented complaints
- [ ] Compare all metrics against the Go/No-Go table above
- [ ] Make the call: Go, Caution (extend 7 days), or No-Go

**If Go:** Begin MVP development per the 5-week build timeline in first-plugin-decision-brief.md. First action: set up WordPress plugin boilerplate with HPOS compatibility.

**If No-Go:** Do not abandon the idea permanently. Archive the data. Move to the WooCommerce Accessibility Auto-Fixer or WooCommerce Smart Sale Scheduler as the first plugin. The competitor complaint catalog and community research remain valuable regardless.
